## 1. Brand Foundation

### Who We Are
Solidigm (思得) — a combination of *solid* and *paradigm*. We are setting the new standard for the storage industry. Formed from SK hynix's acquisition of Intel's NAND and SSD business in 2021.

**Mission:** Unlock data's virtually unlimited potential through advancements in storage technology. We see current limitations as future opportunities.

**Goal:** Become the #1 NAND business by 2030.

### Our Values
- **Customer Inspired** — Listen, understand, anticipate, and partner to solve problems. Honor commitments above all.
- **Invention** — Passionate inventors and technologists unlocking the power of data to fuel human advancement.
- **Excellence** — High-quality, responsible technologies with tenacity and integrity.
- **Openness** — Equitable, respectful, valuing diverse perspectives. Diversity drives innovation.
- **Agility** — Act boldly and with urgency. Nimble, responsive, willing to take intelligent risks.
- **Partnership** — Relationships with teammates, customers, partners, and communities come first.

### Brand Personality: The Seasoned Challenger
Thought leaders with a thoughtful outlook for partners, employees, the environment, and the world. We relish challenges and are inspired by unsolved problems.

### Brand Tone & Voice
- **Optimistic** — Upbeat and enthusiastic devotees of possibility thinking (but not unrealistic day-dreamers)
- **Analytical** — Thoughtful, intelligent and deliberate (but not robotic or staid)
- **Passionate** — Spirited and energized by technology, excited to engage (but not argumentative or confrontational)
- **Genuine** — Authentic, honest and straightforward, human and dynamic (but not trivial or unprepared)
- **Ambitious** — Confident and determined, appreciative of hard work (but not arrogant or dismissive)

## 2. Name & Trademark

### Correct Usage
- Always write as **Solidigm** — no modifications or augmentations
- Use **TM** (™) superscript on first and/or most prominent instance: **Solidigm™**
- After first use, the trademark symbol can be dropped for subsequent mentions in the same document
- **Never use ®** (registered trademark) — not yet registered as of Jan 2026
- **Never use ©** in association with the name or logo

### Possessive Form
- Solidigm **can** be used in possessive form when referring to the company (e.g., "Solidigm's engineering team")
- Solidigm **should NOT** be used in possessive form when referring to the brand
- When in doubt, rephrase to avoid possessive — contact brand@Solidigm.com

### Product Naming Rules
- Use product names **exactly as written** — no modifications, including capitalization
- Do NOT use product name without series name (e.g., never "The P5520" — use the full product name with series)
- Do NOT use possessive form of Solidigm before product name (never "Solidigm's D5-P5336")
- No longer use "(formerly Intel®)" — that transition period has ended

### Chinese Name
- Chinese name: **思得** (Sī Dé / "See Duh")
- First mention in text: **Solidigm™ (思得)**
- Subsequent mentions in same document: can use 思得 alone
- Solidigm written in Sora or Avenir Next LT Pro; 思得 always written in **Noto Sans**

## 3. Logo

### Logo Construction
The Solidigm logo consists of two parts:
- **Brand Mark** — the "S" shape derived from interlocking solid-state drive shapes
- **Word Mark** — "Solidigm" in Sora typeface

The logo DNA is built on **45° and 90° angles** — representing precision, quality, and partnership. The interlocking shapes symbolize our commitment to partnership.

### Logo Versions
1. **Primary Logo** — Brand mark + word mark on a single horizontal line (default, preferred)
2. **Separated Logo** — Brand mark and word mark separated, sharing left or right alignment. 66% minimum height of layout must lie between them. Works best with generous vertical space.
3. **Space-Constrained (Stacked) Logo** — Stacked version for horizontal space constraints, partner logo lockups, or "logo soup" situations. **Requires brand approval before use** — contact brand@solidigm.com
4. **Brand Mark Alone** — Only when the full logo lockup is already visible on the same page/screen

### Logo Color Versions
- **Primary Color (Purple)** — Use on light backgrounds (white, tan, light gray) only
- **White** — Use on dark backgrounds; optically adjusted for dark use — never use on light backgrounds
- **High Contrast (Black)** — Use sparingly, only on medium gray backgrounds where purple or white would fail

### Clear Space
- Minimum clear space around logo = width of the "M" in the word mark
- When aligning to layouts, align to the "M" in Solidigm, not to the TM notation

### Minimum Sizes
- Primary logo: 50px (screen) / 13mm / 0.5 inch (print)
- Remove TM when smaller than 72px or 0.75 inch (primary), 48px or 0.5 inch (brand mark alone or stacked)

### Trademark Placement Within Logo
- TM symbol is always placed **bottom right** of each logo configuration
- Do not move or resize the TM notation within provided logo files
- Do not add or change spacing between TM notation and logo

### Co-Marketing / Partnership Lockups
- Solidigm logo and partner logo should feel **visually balanced** — equal ownership
- Use a **plus sign (+) in Gray** between logos (or light gray for contrast)
- Horizontal lockups preferred; vertical pairings available when space demands
- Solidigm logo must **never appear second or smaller** in co-marketing
- Do not lock up the Solidigm logo with any words or phrases

### Logo Misuse (Never Do These)
- Do not alter the color of the logo — use only approved colors
- Do not add drop shadows or other heavy effects
- Do not distort or stretch
- Do not alter the "S" mark size or position
- Do not create a condensed version
- Do not place white logo on light backgrounds or purple logo on dark backgrounds
- Do not use accent colors (teal, pink, orange) for the logo or as logo background
- Do not have Solidigm logo appear second or smaller in co-marketing

## 5. Typography

### Primary Typeface: Sora
Used for all professional design applications and print. Available free on Google Fonts: https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Sora

**Weights in use:**
- Thin / Extra Light (200) — Hero and headline displays
- Light (300) — Headlines, subheads, body
- Regular (400) — Body copy, long form
- SemiBold (600) — Category labels, buttons, breadcrumbs
- Bold (700) — Breadcrumbs only

### Alternate Typeface for Character Languages: Noto Sans
Use for Chinese (Traditional/Simplified), Japanese, Korean, and other character-based languages where Sora is unsupported. Full family: https://fonts.google.com/noto/fonts

### Microsoft Office Typeface: Avenir Next LT Pro
Use **exclusively** within Microsoft Office tools (Word, PowerPoint, Excel, Outlook). Pre-loaded on Mac and PC.

### Typesetting Rules
- **Headlines**: Sora Thin (200 weight), title case, generous leading, no tight tracking. Avoid all-caps.
- **Subheads**: Extra Light or Light. Title case or sentence case for longer subheads.
- **Body Copy**: Light or Regular. Follow all grammatical standards. Left-aligned.
- **Buttons / Category Labels**: SemiBold (600), 10px, 2px letter-spacing, uppercase
- **Alignment**: Left-align headlines and large blocks of copy. Center-align only in vertical assets or constrained horizontal space. Never center large blocks of text in horizontal layouts.
- **Casing**: Never set headlines or subheads in all uppercase. Use title case (headlines) or sentence case (longer subheads).
- **Punctuation**: Headlines without multiple sentences should have no punctuation. Use punctuation only for multi-sentence headlines.
- **Ampersands**: Acceptable in headlines to save space. Do not use if the word "and" also appears in the same headline. Never in body copy.

### Emphasis Techniques
- **Color accent** — Use primary brand colors for headline emphasis; ensure contrast
- **Headline Frames** — Custom bracket frames scaled to x-height; use opposite corners
- **Quotation Marks** — Custom Solidigm quote marks; minimum clear space = cap-height
- Do NOT use bold weight within a sentence to emphasize individual words
- Do NOT use accent colors for a full headline or large typographic moment

## 6. Graphic Elements

### The Brand Angle
The primary graphic element of the Solidigm brand. Created by extremely tight cropping of the brand mark — resulting in abstract 45° and 90° angle shapes. These angles are the visual language of the brand.

**Rules:**
- Crop must be tight enough that the result is NOT recognizable as the Solidigm logo
- Resulting shape must include at least one 45° or 90° angle
- Do not crop a portion large enough to be identified as the Solidigm "S"
- Do not use crops that protrude awkwardly into the frame

**Approved Brand Angle Color Combinations:**
- Light gray + Solidigm Purple ✓
- Dark Blue + Solidigm Purple ✓
- Light gray + White ✓
- Dark Gray + Medium Gray ✓

**Brand Angle Color Misuse:**
- Do NOT use accent colors (Teal, Pink, Orange) in dominant Brand Angle situations
- Do NOT use two accent colors together in the Brand Angle

### Spot Illustration Style
An extension of the logo and icon system. Key characteristics:
- **Single line weight** — no fills; strokes only (matching the "S" brand mark stroke weight)
- Must include at least one **45° branded corner** (90° inside, two 45° outside angles)
- Squares can be used in grids to convey data or as stylistic elements
- **Open areas** — one edge or area should be left open (unconstraining, limitless possibilities)
- Slightly more fidelity than icons

## 7. Iconography

### Design Principles
Icons are derived from the DNA of the Solidigm logo:
- **45° and 90° angles** — all icons built on this grid
- **Single line weight** — no fills, no gradients
- **72px × 72px container** — icons should not protrude from this container
- Include a **brand corner** (90° inner angle, 45° outer angles) where possible
- Minimum size: 16px (screen) / 6mm (print)
- Maximum size: 200px (screen) / 75mm (print) — larger requires brand approval

### Icon Color Usage
- Primary: white or gray icons on brand purple backgrounds, with teal/orange accent if needed
- Secondary: white/gray on dark blue or dark gray backgrounds
- Light backgrounds allow both white and dark icons
- Do NOT use insufficient contrast combinations (e.g., light purple on purple)
- Do NOT overuse accent colors

### Icon Misuse
- Do not use more than one line weight in a single icon
- Do not add gradients
- Do not add additional elements to existing icons
- Do not overlap multiple icons
- Do not use icons inline with text
- Do not resize multiple icons to different sizes in the same set

## 8. Photography

### Photography Philosophy
Authentic, editorial, documentary-style photography. Honest storytelling. Never overly staged.

**Look for:**
- Authentic moments and emotions
- Documentary / fly-on-the-wall feel
- Unique angles
- Diversity of age and ethnicity

### Common Threads
- **Diversity** — Reflect the real world; culturally diverse people and subject matter
- **Authenticity** — Unposed, editorial feel; never studio-staged
- **Hierarchy** — Clear composition; use focus to tell a concise story
- **Natural lighting** — Avoid heavily colored lighting effects
- **In-the-moment** — Problem solvers engaged in tasks, unaware of camera
- **Realism** — Realistic expressions; avoid overly staged emotions

### Photo Retouching
**Level 1 (Standard):** Subtle curves adjustment to neutral white balance. Slightly exaggerated light sources. Very soft Solidigm Purple added to highlights. Maintains natural skin tones. Use for almost all materials.

**Level 2 (Stylized):** More dramatic. Purple appears to emanate from light sources. Use for hero/standalone pieces only.

**Never:** "Purple wash" photos. Make color adjustments too noticeable. Use obviously colorized results.

### Photography Subjects
- Data centers and server environments (use unique angles, abstract/textural when appropriate)
- Problem solvers actively engaged (singular or group)
- "In the moment" — contemplation, looking ahead, never directly at camera
- Partnership / groups — documentary style, real environments, diverse teams
- Technology impact — everyday technology moments enabled by data storage

### Industries to Represent
CDN & Service Providers, Streaming Services, Ride Sharing, Creative Industries, Film Production, Medical Imaging, AI & Connectivity, Green Energy

### What to Avoid in Photography
- Overly purple images / obvious color casting
- Off-brand colors (non-Solidigm palette)
- Unnatural lighting / studio setups with fake lighting
- Overly staged portraiture
- Mismatched or overemphasized emotions
- Off-brand photo effects or filters
- Focusing on tech unrelated to storage
- Underemphasizing the subject

## 9. Generative AI Rules

### Hard Rules (No Exceptions)
- **NEVER** incorporate any element of our logo into any generative AI image
- **NEVER** incorporate the Solidigm name into any generative AI image
- **NEVER** use gen AI to develop a logo, lockup, icon, or any visual meant to "brand" a program, team, resource, event, or document
- All external-facing generative AI imagery **requires review and approval** by the Brand and Creative teams before use

### Required Disclaimer
Always add: **"AI-generated"** or **"Generated with Copilot"** (or whichever platform was used). The Copilot icon alone is not sufficient.

### Usage Guidelines
- Avoid overly generic, busy, tropey, or cartoony images
- Avoid AI-generated humans or animals
- Avoid anything requiring precision or accuracy (SSDs, internal components)
- If using purple in AI images, ensure it is one of our brand purples
- AI images complement a concept; they do not replace detailed graphic design work
- Only use AI tools as permitted in the Corporate AI Usage and Development Information Security Policy

### Approval Process
Submit to the Brand and Creative teams via the AI imagery approval form. Reviewed in the Gen AI Review meeting every other week.

## 10. Charts & Data

### Chart Color Usage
Lead with brand colors. Use accents as highlights only. Ensure legibility between chart sections, type colors, and backgrounds.

**For light backgrounds:**
- Grid lines: 1pt gray
- Text: Dark Blue (unless on dark-colored wedge/section — then use white)

**For dark backgrounds:**
- Grid lines: 1pt white
- Text: White (unless on light-colored wedge/section — then use dark blue)

### Chart Color Rules
- Gradients allowed, but stick to tints or shades of a **single** hue — never mix different hues in the same gradient
- Do NOT use Electric Pink or Electric Teal in charts — difficult on eyes in small, detail-oriented placements
- Do NOT concurrently use gradients and mix different hues from the palette

### Chart-Only Colors (Do Not Use Elsewhere)
- Medium Blue `#475ada`
- Dark Teal `#187e7b`

### Table Styles
- 1pt spacing between columns; alternate colors between rows (no row spacing)
- Highlighted columns may use a thicker border
- Keep data tables minimal — choose 1 font weight and size for row headers, one for data
- Thin rules for row separation (roughly same weight as typography)
- Legal/disclaimers: small but legible, below chart, left-aligned

## 12. Do Nots (Hard Constraints)

These are absolute constraints for any AI-generated output. Violating these risks brand damage, trademark issues, or accessibility failures.

### Identity & Trademark
- ❌ Never generate or alter the Solidigm logo in any way
- ❌ Never use the ® symbol — use ™ only
- ❌ Never use © in association with the name or logo
- ❌ Never incorporate Solidigm's name or logo into AI-generated imagery
- ❌ Never create a bespoke logo for a team, program, or event — use department lockup guidelines

### Color
- ❌ Never use accent colors (Electric Teal, Pink, Orange) as dominant or primary design elements
- ❌ Never use Electric Teal or Electric Pink outside of approved creative design contexts
- ❌ Never use chart-specific colors (Medium Blue, Dark Teal) outside of charts/graphs
- ❌ Never use high-contrast gradients mixing multiple hues
- ❌ Never "purple wash" photography
- ❌ Never break WCAG 2.1 AA contrast standards (minimum 4.5:1)
- ❌ Never use the white logo on light backgrounds or the purple logo on dark backgrounds

### Typography
- ❌ Never set headlines or subheads in all uppercase
- ❌ Never use bold weight mid-sentence to emphasize individual words
- ❌ Never use a font other than Sora (non-Latin), Noto Sans (character languages), or Avenir Next LT Pro (Microsoft Office)
- ❌ Never center-align large blocks of text in horizontal layouts
- ❌ Never use the possessive form of Solidigm when referring to the brand (only the company)

### Logo Usage
- ❌ Never alter, distort, stretch, or recolor the logo
- ❌ Never add drop shadows or heavy effects to the logo
- ❌ Never have the Solidigm logo appear second or smaller in co-marketing
- ❌ Never use the stacked (space-constrained) logo without brand approval
- ❌ Never use the brand mark entirely alone without prior brand approval

### Generative AI
- ❌ Never use gen AI to create logos, lockups, icons, or branded visual elements
- ❌ Never publish external AI-generated imagery without Brand and Creative team approval
- ❌ Never omit the "AI-generated" disclaimer from AI imagery

### Iconography
- ❌ Never use gradients in icons
- ❌ Never use more than one line weight in an icon
- ❌ Never use icons inline with text
- ❌ Never display icons larger than 200px without brand approval
