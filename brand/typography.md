# Typography

## Primary Typeface: Sora
Used for all professional design applications and print. Available free on Google Fonts: https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Sora

**Weights in use:**
- Thin / Extra Light (200) — Hero and headline displays
- Light (300) — Headlines, subheads, body
- Regular (400) — Body copy, long form
- SemiBold (600) — Category labels, buttons, breadcrumbs
- Bold (700) — Breadcrumbs only

## Alternate Typeface for Character Languages: Noto Sans
Use for Chinese (Traditional/Simplified), Japanese, Korean, and other character-based languages where Sora is unsupported.

## Microsoft Office Typeface: Avenir Next LT Pro
Use **exclusively** within Microsoft Office tools (Word, PowerPoint, Excel, Outlook). Pre-loaded on Mac and PC.

## Typesetting Rules
- **Headlines**: Sora Thin (200 weight), title case, generous leading, no tight tracking. Avoid all-caps.
- **Subheads**: Extra Light or Light. Title case or sentence case for longer subheads.
- **Body Copy**: Light or Regular. Follow all grammatical standards. Left-aligned.
- **Buttons / Category Labels**: SemiBold (600), 10px, 2px letter-spacing, uppercase
- **Alignment**: Left-align headlines and large blocks of copy. Center-align only in vertical assets or constrained horizontal space. Never center large blocks of text in horizontal layouts.
- **Casing**: Never set headlines or subheads in all uppercase. Use title case (headlines) or sentence case (longer subheads).
- **Punctuation**: Headlines without multiple sentences should have no punctuation. Use punctuation only for multi-sentence headlines.
- **Ampersands**: Acceptable in headlines to save space. Do not use if the word "and" also appears in the same headline. Never in body copy.

## Emphasis Techniques
- **Color accent** — Use primary brand colors for headline emphasis; ensure contrast
- **Headline Frames** — Custom bracket frames scaled to x-height; use opposite corners
- **Quotation Marks** — Custom Solidigm quote marks; minimum clear space = cap-height
- Do NOT use bold weight within a sentence to emphasize individual words
- Do NOT use accent colors for a full headline or large typographic moment
