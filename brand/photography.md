# Photography

## Photography Philosophy
Authentic, editorial, documentary-style photography. Honest storytelling. Never overly staged.

**Look for:**
- Authentic moments and emotions
- Documentary / fly-on-the-wall feel
- Unique angles
- Diversity of age and ethnicity

## Common Threads
- **Diversity** — Reflect the real world; culturally diverse people and subject matter
- **Authenticity** — Unposed, editorial feel; never studio-staged
- **Hierarchy** — Clear composition; use focus to tell a concise story
- **Natural lighting** — Avoid heavily colored lighting effects
- **In-the-moment** — Problem solvers engaged in tasks, unaware of camera
- **Realism** — Realistic expressions; avoid overly staged emotions

## Photo Retouching
**Level 1 (Standard):** Subtle curves adjustment to neutral white balance. Slightly exaggerated light sources. Very soft Solidigm Purple added to highlights. Maintains natural skin tones. Use for almost all materials.

**Level 2 (Stylized):** More dramatic. Purple appears to emanate from light sources. Use for hero/standalone pieces only.

**Never:** "Purple wash" photos. Make color adjustments too noticeable. Use obviously colorized results.

## Photography Subjects
- Data centers and server environments (use unique angles, abstract/textural when appropriate)
- Problem solvers actively engaged (singular or group)
- "In the moment" — contemplation, looking ahead, never directly at camera
- Partnership / groups — documentary style, real environments, diverse teams
- Technology impact — everyday technology moments enabled by data storage

## Industries to Represent
CDN & Service Providers, Streaming Services, Ride Sharing, Creative Industries, Film Production, Medical Imaging, AI & Connectivity, Green Energy

## What to Avoid
- Overly purple images / obvious color casting
- Off-brand colors (non-Solidigm palette)
- Unnatural lighting / studio setups with fake lighting
- Overly staged portraiture
- Mismatched or overemphasized emotions
- Off-brand photo effects or filters
- Focusing on tech unrelated to storage
- Underemphasizing the subject
