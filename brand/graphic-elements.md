# Graphic Elements

## The Brand Angle
The primary graphic element of the Solidigm brand. Created by extremely tight cropping of the brand mark — resulting in abstract 45° and 90° angle shapes. These angles are the visual language of the brand.

**Rules:**
- Crop must be tight enough that the result is NOT recognizable as the Solidigm logo
- Resulting shape must include at least one 45° or 90° angle
- Do not crop a portion large enough to be identified as the Solidigm "S"
- Do not use crops that protrude awkwardly into the frame

**Approved Brand Angle Color Combinations:**
- Light gray + Solidigm Purple ✓
- Dark Blue + Solidigm Purple ✓
- Light gray + White ✓
- Dark Gray + Medium Gray ✓

**Brand Angle Color Misuse:**
- Do NOT use accent colors (Teal, Pink, Orange) in dominant Brand Angle situations
- Do NOT use two accent colors together in the Brand Angle

## Spot Illustration Style
An extension of the logo and icon system. Key characteristics:
- **Single line weight** — no fills; strokes only (matching the "S" brand mark stroke weight)
- Must include at least one **45° branded corner** (90° inside, two 45° outside angles)
- Squares can be used in grids to convey data or as stylistic elements
- **Open areas** — one edge or area should be left open (unconstraining, limitless possibilities)
- Slightly more fidelity than icons
